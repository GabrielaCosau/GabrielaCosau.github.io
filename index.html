Hello there!

My name is Gabriela Cosau and I am an UX/UI Designer with the main focus on mobile app interface building. Another area in which I an highly skilled is digital art and illustrations and I developed my art style abound certain shapes and colors. I started my UX/UI adventure since one year ago and in the last two years I've been practicing digital art at a higher level. All my UX/UI projects are based on research with real people and scenarios, including surveys, user and usability testing. The interfaces are data driven as well as aesthetically pleasing. I'm passionate with my work and I'm looking forward to future design challenges.

About me

   I'm a young designer and artist who felt isn't afraid to make a huge shit their career path and start again from zero. I truly believe in my calling and I know I have a lot to offer back.
   I started as a confused 18 year old not sure what to do with her life and chose to study foreign languages (English and German) and gave 100% of myself hoping I'll find it enjoyable. Spoiler alert!!! I didn't. However I finished my studies, did also a master in the same field and participated in tow study mobilities abroad in the meantime.

   In the last year of masters I realized I don't enjoy what I'm doing and I don't want to make my life about it. Thus I dug deep to find what I love, and after a lot of thinking, research, and trying new things I came across UX/UI Design and I knew this is what I want my career to be about. I started to read more and look for tutorials but I knew it wasn't enough, so I enrolled for a four month UX/UI Design course at "The Informal School of IT". During this time I learned the bases of this filed and a bit more, I created case studies and built my portfolio. After the course ended I continued to learn on my own, created new and exciting projects, and participated at a Digital Design Training at Endava. At some point I felt the need to round my education and decided to start a new masters degree related to this field, namely "Digital Media and Game Studies" where I'm a student today.
